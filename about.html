How I Got Here

As a child, I was fascinated by how things functioned. I spent hours disassembling gadgets to see how they worked. This curiosity naturally led me to study electrical engineering. I enrolled at Kenyatta University and majored in electrical engineering. My favourite subjects were circuit design and robotics, which allowed me to be creative and innovative. One of my proudest accomplishments was managing a team endeavour to create a solar-powered charging station for distant places. My internship with KenGen was a game changer. Working on real-world projects helped me refine my talents and understand the value of teamwork and communication. This encounter cemented my enthusiasm for the field and laid the groundwork for my professional career. Since then, I've worked on a variety of intriguing projects.Looking ahead, I'm excited about the future of renewable energy and smart technology. I aspire to contribute to sustainable solutions that make a positive impact on the world
